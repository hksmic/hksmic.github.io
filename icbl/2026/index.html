Call for papers

Call for papers

You are cordially invited to participate in the 19th International Conference on Blended Learning (ICBL 2026) to be held at Peking University in Beijing, China during 28 to 31 July 2026. Blended learning has long been a promising approach to integrating traditional teaching with innovative digital means. However, the educational landscape is currently undergoing a seismic shift driven by rapid advancements in artificial intelligence. The emergence of large language models (LLMs) and intelligent pedagogical agents is redefining the boundaries of what is possible in personalised education. ICBL 2026 seeks to explore how these cutting-edge technologies are transforming blended learning experience and aims to provide a premier platform for knowledge exchange and experience sharing among researchers, educators, and practitioners in the field. The conference theme is “Blended Learning: Innovations for Future Education.” Original papers reporting the latest research findings and novel applications of blended learning are welcome. ICBL 2026 covers a broad range of topics related to blended learning including, but not limited to:

  • Adaptive Learning
  • Application of AI in Learning
  • AR, VR, and MR in Learning
  • Assessment for Blended Learning
  • Computer-Supported Collaborative Learning
  • Content Development for Blended Learning
  • Enriching Learning Experience with Blended Learning
  • Experiences in Blended Learning
  • Game-based Learning and Gamification
  • Improved Flexibility of Learning Processes
  • Interactive Blended Learning Systems
  • Learning Analytics and Academic Advising
  • Mobile and Ubiquitous Learning
  • Open Educational Resources (OER)
  • Organisational Frameworks for Blended Learning
  • Pedagogical and Psychological Issues
  • Personalised Learning
  • Practices in Borderless Education
  • Smart Learning Environments
  • Strategies for Blended Learning

Paper Submission

Original papers should be written in English and submitted by 31 January 2026.
Format: Your paper should follow the Springer’s Lecture Notes in Computer Science (LNCS) format.
Length: Submissions should be approximately 12 pages in length.
Publication: The Conference Proceedings are planned to be published by Springer under the Lecture Notes in Computer Science Series (subject to confirmation).
Awards: Best/Excellent Paper Awards will be granted to authors of papers with exceptional merit.
ISET 2026 Recommendation: ICBL works closely with its sister conference, the International Symposium on Educational Technology (ISET). During the review process, the joint Program Committee may identify submissions that, while of high quality, are better aligned with the specific scope and thematic focus of ISET 2026. In such cases, authors may be invited to transfer their submission to ISET 2026. Authors accepting this recommendation will be required to revise their papers in accordance with ISET 2026 submission requirements and must address the comments provided by the ICBL reviewers before final acceptance.
Conference Registration and Attendance: To ensure the integrity of the conference program and proceedings, the following registration policies will be strictly enforced: (1) Each accepted paper must be accompanied by at least one full conference registration by the registration deadline; (2) It is mandatory for at least one author of an accepted paper to attend the conference in person to present their work; and (3) Papers that are not registered or are not presented in person during the scheduled session will be removed from the final conference proceedings. Exceptions to this policy will only be made in extreme, unforeseen circumstances, and require prior written approval from the Technical Programme Chairs.
