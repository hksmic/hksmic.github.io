University Leadership in AI-Driven Digital Transformation: A European Perspective Through the TOP Model

Abstract. The rapid advancement of artificial intelligence (AI) is fundamentally reshaping higher education systems worldwide, compelling universities to reconsider their missions, governance structures, and leadership models. This article examines the evolving role of university leadership in the context of AI-driven digital transformation, adopting the Technology–Organization–Pedagogy (TOP) model, developed by the Edul@b Research Group at the Universitat Oberta de Catalunya (UOC), as an analytical framework. Grounded in the European higher education tradition, with its particular commitments to quality assurance, inclusivity, and public value, the paper identifies the principal challenges and opportunities associated with integrating AI into teaching, learning, and institutional governance. Drawing on a range of foundational scholarship, including UNESCO’s Guidance for Generative AI in Education and Research (2023), systematic reviews of AI in higher education (Crompton & Burke, 2023), critical perspectives on the limits of AI (Selwyn, 2024; Molenaar, 2022; Luckin, 2018), a literature review on e-leadership for technology-enhanced learning (Arnold & Sangrà, 2018), and the Bologna Digital 2020 White Paper (Rampelt, Orr, & Knoth, 2019), the article argues that effective leadership must move beyond technological adoption toward systemic transformation, emphasizing ethical responsibility, organizational culture change, and genuinely human-centered approaches to educational design. Strategic directions for university leaders are proposed within each dimension of the TOP model, and the relevance of a productive East-West dialogue, particularly between European quality assurance traditions and China’s leadership in AI development and deployment, is explored.

Education Technology and Artificial Intelligence (AI) Empowers Education: Cases from Peking University, China

Abstract. Information Communication Technology and Artificial intelligence technology have made remarkable achievements in the past decade, and these breakthroughs have provided impetus for the development and transformation of education, for example to provide individualized teaching and learning, to implement the policy documents issued by the central government on the assessment reform of primary and secondary school students and reducing the burden and increasing the efficiency. This speech will introduce two AI systems designed by the speaker’s team from Peking University, as well as their application in education and the corresponding effect evaluation: The English instruction System CSIEC (Computer Simulation in Educational Communication) V2.0, and the intelligent mathematical assessment and tutoring system MIATS V2.0.

GenAI Empowered Interdisciplinary Deep Learning: Theoretical Frameworks and Technical Pathways in Blended Environments

Abstract. Interdisciplinary learning has emerged as an effective pedagogical approach for cultivating learners’ interdisciplinary competencies and promoting holistic educational outcomes in blended learning environments. However, interdisciplinary learning continues to be constrained by epistemological tensions, structural fragmentation, and delayed evaluation feedback, which hinder its progression from mere disciplinary juxtaposition to genuinely integrated, deep interdisciplinary knowledge construction. This study systematically reviews the conceptual foundations of interdisciplinary learning and interdisciplinary deep learning, and conceptualizes the structural dimensions of interdisciplinary competencies encompassing interdisciplinary knowledge, thinking, practice, and values. A four-level cognitive progression mechanism is further explicated, comprising descriptive construction, relevance construction, systemic construction, and innovative construction. From the perspective of generative artificial intelligence (GenAI) empowerment, four technological pathways are proposed to facilitate interdisciplinary deep learning: problem representation based on generative contexts, semantic network connectivity under distributed cognition, resource integration and systematic cognitive modeling, and meaning negotiation and critical dialogue regulation. This study offers both theoretical insights and technological implications for advancing the high-quality and intelligent transformation of interdisciplinary learning in the context of educational digitalization.

Agentic AI Unleashed: Power, Promise, and Ethical Frontiers

Abstract. Artificial Intelligence is evolving from passive tools to autonomous agents capable of making decisions, pursuing goals, and interacting dynamically with complex environments. This keynote explores the transformative potential of Agentic AI—systems endowed with agency—and the profound ethical questions they raise. We will examine real-world applications across healthcare, education, finance, and scientific discovery, highlighting how agentic systems can accelerate innovation and reshape human-AI collaboration. At the same time, we confront critical challenges: accountability for autonomous decisions, transparency in algorithmic reasoning, and the alignment of machine objectives with human values. By mapping the ethical frontiers and proposing frameworks for responsible deployment, this session invites researchers, policymakers, and industry leaders to envision a future where agentic AI amplifies human potential without compromising societal trust.
